***

Differentiating Similar Entities for Exams:

1. <font color="red">Nasal Bone vs. Mandible:</font> The Nasal Bone is the #1 most common facial fracture; Mandible is the #2 most common.
2. <font color="red">Cricothyrotomy vs. Tracheostomy:</font> Cricothyrotomy is a temporary emergency first-aid; Tracheostomy is the definitive hospital-based airway.
3. <font color="red">V2 vs. V3 Nerve Injury:</font> V2 (Maxillary) injury causes upper lip/cheek numbness (common in ZMC/Maxilla fractures); V3 (Mandibular) injury causes lower lip/chin numbness (common in Mandibular body fractures).
4. <font color="red">Favorable vs. Unfavorable Mandible Fracture:</font> Favorable fractures are held together by muscle pull; Unfavorable fractures are pulled apart and require surgery (ORIF).
5. <font color="red">LeFort I vs. II vs. III:</font> LeFort I is the palate only; LeFort II is the pyramidal/midface; LeFort III is the entire face separating from the skull.
6. <font color="red">Trismus (Mandible) vs. Trismus (ZMC):</font> In Mandible fractures, trismus is due to pain/displacement; in ZMC fractures, it is often due to the zygomatic arch impinging on the Coronoid Process.
7. <font color="red">Hypoglobus vs. Enophthalmos:</font> Hypoglobus is an "up/down" displacement (downward); Enophthalmos is a "front/back" displacement (sunken eyes).
8. <font color="red">Manual reduction vs. ORIF timing:</font> Nasal fractures should be closed-reduced within <2 weeks; after 2 weeks, they require Open Reduction (ORIF).
9. <font color="red">Forced Duction Test Results:</font> If the eye is restricted/cannot be moved manually, it is muscle entrapment; if the eye moves easily manually but not on its own, it is nerve paralysis.
10. <font color="red">Submental vs. Submandibular Drainage:</font> Submental drains the chin/floor of mouth; Submandibular drains the medial face/upper lip.
11. <font color="red">Lateral Pterygoid vs. Other Masticatory Muscles:</font> Lateral Pterygoid "Opens" the mouth; Masseter, Temporalis, and Medial Pterygoid "Close" the mouth.
12. <font color="red">Towne’s View vs. Panoramic X-Ray:</font> Towne’s is best for the Condyle; Panoramic (Panorex) is best for the Mandibular Body.
13. <font color="red">Water’s View vs. Other X-rays:</font> Water’s view is the specific X-ray used to see the Teardrop sign in orbital/ZMC fractures.
14. <font color="red">Functional vs. Cosmetic Indications:</font> In ZMC fractures, diplopia and trismus (functional) are primary indicators for surgery, while enophthalmos (cosmetic) is secondary.
15. <font color="red">Cricothyrotomy Tube vs. Method:</font> Cricothyrotomy is performed between the thyroid and cricoid cartilages; Tracheostomy is performed lower in the neck (tracheal rings).

### <center>NECK DISSECTION</center>

1.  **Level IA**: Submental (Floor of mouth, lower lip).
2.  **Level IB**: Submandibular gland (Oral cavity).
3.  **Level II, III, IV**: Along the SCM (Oropharynx, Larynx, Thyroid).
4.  **Level V**: Posterior neck (Nasopharynx).
5.  **Level VI**: Central compartment (Thyroid, subglottic larynx).

* (Neck Dissection) <font color="red">**Therapeutic neck dissection**</font> is performed when neck nodes are clinically evident/positive.
* (Neck Dissection) <font color="red">**Elective neck dissection**</font> is performed in node-negative patients who have a > 20% risk of occult metastasis (e.g., Supraglottic CA).
* (Neck Dissection) <font color="red">**Radical Neck Dissection (RND)**</font> involves removal of Levels I-V plus the **SCM, IJV, and CN XI**.
* (Neck Dissection) <font color="red">**Modified Neck Dissection (MRND)**</font> involves removal of Levels I-V but preserves one or more of the three structures (SCM, IJV, CN XI).
* (Neck Dissection) <font color="red">**Selective Neck Dissection (SND)**</font> involves removing only a specific selection of nodal levels (e.g., Level VI for early thyroid CA).
* (Neck Dissection) If an elective dissection reveals a **positive node**, the procedure must be converted to a <font color="red">**Radical or Modified Radical Neck Dissection**</font>.

### <center>DIFFERENTIATING CONFUSING ENTITIES</center>

* <font color="red">**Papillary vs. Follicular Thyroid CA**</font>: Papillary primarily spreads through **lymphatics** (local nodes), whereas Follicular spreads **hematogenously** (distant organs like bone/liver).
* <font color="red">**Thyroid Node Staging vs. Other Head/Neck Staging**</font>: Thyroid N-staging is based on **location** (N1a central vs. N1b lateral), while Head and Neck N-staging is based on **size/count** (N1 <3cm, N2 3-6cm, N3 >6cm).
* <font color="red">**MEN 2A vs. MEN 2B**</font>: Both involve Medullary Thyroid CA, but MEN 2A includes **parathyroid hyperplasia**, and 2B does not.
* <font color="red">**Supraglottic vs. Glottic vs. Subglottic**</font>: Supraglottic = **Dysphagia** (swallowing), Glottic = **Hoarseness** (voice), Subglottic = **Dyspnea** (breathing).
* <font color="red">**Pleomorphic Adenoma vs. Warthin's Tumor**</font>: Pleomorphic is the **most common** and is "mixed" (solid/lobulated); Warthin's is **cystic** and frequently **bilateral/associated with smokers**.
* <font color="red">**Radical vs. Modified vs. Selective Neck Dissection**</font>: Radical removes **3 specific structures** (SCM/IJV/CN XI); Modified **saves** them; Selective removes **only specific levels** (1-3 levels).
* <font color="red">**House-Brackmann Grade III vs. Grade IV**</font>: Grade III has **complete eye closure** with effort; Grade IV has **incomplete eye closure**.
* <font color="red">**Elective vs. Therapeutic Neck Dissection**</font>: Elective is for **clinically negative (N0)** necks with high risk; Therapeutic is for **clinically positive (N+)** nodes.
* <font color="red">**Thyroid vs. Salivary Staging (T2)**</font>: For Thyroid, T2 is **2-4 cm**; For Salivary, T2 is **≥2 cm to ≤4 cm**. Both are identical in size range, but Thyroid Stage III starts > 4cm within the gland.
* <font color="red">**Submandibular vs. Parotid Salivation**</font>: Submandibular produces **mucoid** saliva (common for stones); Parotid produces **serous** saliva (active during eating).
* <font color="red">**Sublingual vs. Parotid Malignancy Rate**</font>: A sublingual mass is **70% likely to be malignant**, whereas a parotid mass is **70% likely to be benign**.
* <font color="red">**Planned vs. Salvage Neck Dissection**</font>: Planned is done **regardless of response** to radiation; Salvage is done for **residual or persistent** nodes that failed to resolve.
* <font color="red">**N2b vs. N2c Neck Nodes**</font>: N2b is **multiple nodes on the same side** (ipsilateral); N2c is **nodes on both sides** (bilateral) or the opposite side (contralateral).
* <font color="red">**Inspiratory vs. Expiratory Stridor**</font>: Inspiratory stridor usually points to a **supraglottic** obstruction; expiratory stridor must be differentiated from **wheezing**.
* <font color="red">**Filipino thyroid CA vs. Western Guidelines**</font>: PH surgeons perform **total thyroidectomy at > 1 cm** due to higher aggressiveness; Western surgeons often perform **lobectomy up to 4 cm**.
